November 15: A Programmatic Approach to Pre-Revenue Pharmacofinance & Clinical Trial Planning

RSVP BELOW!

We are back in person (and virtual) this month welcoming repeat speaker Michael Kane to talk about drug development and financing.

Thank you to BrainStation for hosting us.

After the talk we are giving away two free tickets (either virtual or in-person) at random for the R in Government & Public Sector Conference taking place November 30 - December 2.

Everybody attending must RSVP through the registration form at nyhackr.org. Virtual attendees are free and will receive the stream link after registration while there is a small charge for in-person attendees to cover the pizza.

About the Talk: Drug development is driven by the expected payout of successful drugs over its lifetime. An informed decision to invest in, acquire, or plan a trial for a new compound considers the likelihood the compound will reach regulatory approval along with downstream investments and future profits. However, drug development markets are still relatively inefficient, and sponsors lack tools and capabilities for evaluating development risk. In this talk I’ll provide an overview of the drug development space and show how we have been using publicly available data and R with our ctrialsgov package to quantify risk in the space.

About Michael: Michael Kane is an Assistant Professor of Biostatistics at Yale University along and Founder and Scientific Advisor at Telperian Inc. His research focus is on machine learning methods that incorporate reproducibility, interpretability and uncertainty in prediction accuracy. His current domain focus is on characterizing patient heterogeneity in clinical trials.
